About Me

How I got to where I am

Born and raised in Manchester, England, I moved to the States in 2014 and spent a year living in North Carolina until I came to my senses and moved across the country to San Francisco.

I used my 20s to explore my career options and find something I was truly passionate about. At 30 years old, I've finally settled on a direction I want to take in my life and am pursuing a career in web development.

Skills

The things I've learned on my journey
Hairdressing

In England, I was a fully trained and qualified unisex hair stylist. I studied for two years at Accrington & Rossendale College, completing level 2 and 3 qualifications with excellent grades. I went on to work for 4 years in a small salon in my home town, before moving to America, where all of my qualifications were not recognized.

Hospitality

After moving to America in 2014, I quickly found myself a job at a nearby Hilton hotel as a front desk agent. I already had experience dealing with people from my hairdressing job, and I picked up the Property Management software with relative ease. 6 months later, I moved to San Francisco and struggled finding a hotel that would hire me with so little experience.

Driving

My next couple of jobs were centered around my driving skills. I was a Pickup and Delivery Specialist for Shift, then I moved on to driving for Lyft for 6 months. It was during my time with Lyft that I found the job I'd stick with for the next 4 years.

Warehouse Management & Training

I started working for Omni Projects Inc. around September 2016. I started as a delivery driver, but quickly worked my way up the ranks to Shift Lead. After some time in this role, I took on additional responsibilities as the lead trainer. Working at a startup where all training was 'tribal knowledge', it was my responsibility to create documentation for processes and procedures for each department within the warehouse, and then create and implement a training program to ensure reliable training of new hires.

I learned a lot about management, warehouse operations, training, and how to properly load, unload, and drive a transit van during my time with Omni.
